## Standards

### General

A standard is a formally defined set of principles, criteria, and/or rules that establish requirements, methods, or expectations for how a particular activity, process, measurement, or behaviour is to be performed, assessed, or interpreted. Standards are designed to promote consistency, and thereby comparability, across different actors and contexts.

The Independent Impact Platform recognises several types of standards. These include, but are not limited to, the following:

- Standards of expertise
- Conduct standards
- Financial accounting standards
- Impact accounting standards
- Quality standards
- Measurement precision standards

Any standards body is allowed to operate one or more standards on the platform provided that they adhere to the rules set out below.

A standards body who wishes to operate an impact accounting standard on the platform must see to it that they and their standard comply with the following:

1. The standard must be demonstrably relevant to anthropogenic impact accounting.
2. The standards body must provide the following artefacts for the standard:

- a set of principles
- a glossary or concept scheme
- measures (questions) to guide activity owners towards adherence to their principles
- measures (questions) to guide reviewers in their evaluation of an activity's adherence to the standard's principles

The platform's review process is designed to award high scores to activities that adhere to the standard's principles.

### Conduct standards

The only conduct standard in operation on the platform is Independent Impact's own reputation-based conduct standard. No other conduct standards are accommodated. For more information on the II conduct standard, please refer to the chapter on "Agents."
