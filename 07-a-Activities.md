## Activities

### General

In the context of Independent Impact, the term "activity" is used in accordance with its definition in [AIAO](https://aiaont.github.io/aiao/aiao.html#cls--Activity): "An event that is orchestrated by an agent." In simple language this means that an activity is a something that an agent does on purpose.

Any user of the platform can create a new activity, provided that they have sufficient Conduct Reputation to do so, and they have provided enough information about themselves to allow other parties to hold them legally accountable for the consequences of their activity.

The platform supports the creation and management of activities related to anthropogenic impact across a wide range of contexts. These may include, but are not limited to, voluntary carbon offset initiatives, compliance market mechanisms, charitable contributions, and corporate ESG or CSR programmes.

### Activity Scoring System

The Activity Scoring System (ActSco for short) is designed to measure how well an activity adheres to the principles of some standard. Each standards body operating on the platform is responsible for drafting the criteria against which an activity should be reviewed to determine the degree to which it meets their standard. Correlatively, each activity owner is required to choose the standard against which their activity should be evaluated.
